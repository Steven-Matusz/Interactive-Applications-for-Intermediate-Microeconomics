Market Equilibrium Details
Variable Value Notes
Market Price (P*) — Equilibrium price at intersection of supply & demand
Firm Output (q*) — Each firm produces where P = MC
Market Output (Q*) — Total = (number of firms) × (firm output)
Marginal Cost (MC) — Should equal price at optimum
Average Total Cost (ATC) — P vs ATC determines profit/loss
Average Variable Cost (AVC) — P vs AVC determines shutdown decision
Economic Profit per Firm — (P − ATC) × q*
Long-Run Expectation — Entry/exit pressure in long run

📚 Key Concepts

Short-Run Supply Curve Each firm's MC curve above AVC. Adding up all firms gives market supply.
Entry & Exit Rule Profit > 0 → firms enter → supply shifts right → price falls. Loss < 0 → firms exit → supply shifts left → price rises.
Long-Run Equilibrium Economic profit = 0. Price equals minimum ATC. No incentive to enter or exit.
Shutdown Rule A firm shuts down when P < min AVC (it cannot cover variable costs). Fixed costs are sunk in the short run.
