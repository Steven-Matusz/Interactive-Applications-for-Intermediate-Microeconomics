This diagram shows leisure hours (0–2,700) on the horizontal axis and annual income ($0–$40,000) on the vertical axis. The grey dashed line is the budget constraint without welfare benefits. The red line shows the welfare budget constraint, which is kinked: to the right of the benefit cut-off point the household receives the full base benefit (flat segment); to the left the effective slope reflects the net wage after benefit reduction. The grey dot marks the no-welfare choice; the red dot marks the choice with the current welfare policy. At 100% reduction rate, the household maximises utility by taking all leisure (2,500 hours) and living on benefits alone — the poverty trap.
