🎓 Why Does Bundling Work?

Bundling is most profitable when customers have negatively correlated preferences — one customer values Product A highly but Product B less, while another has opposite preferences.

  • Separate pricing — best when customers have similar, homogeneous preferences.
  • Pure bundling — smooths out diverse preferences; customers pay similar totals even when individual valuations differ. Captures more surplus.
  • Mixed bundling — charges premium prices to high-value customers for individual items, while using the bundle to capture customers with moderate valuations for both.

The key insight: bundling aggregates willingness to pay, reducing the variance across customers and letting the firm set a higher price without excluding buyers.
