Optimal rule: Each period, set P* = (demand intercept + MC) / 2. This is the midpoint between the current demand intercept and marginal cost—the profit-maximizing price given that period's residual demand.

💡 The Coase Conjecture: If consumers anticipate that prices will fall, they wait to buy later. This forces the monopolist to cut prices faster, potentially driving price toward marginal cost. Real firms combat this with price guarantees, annual model refreshes, and leasing.
