📚 Key Concept: Monopolistic Competition

In short-run equilibrium, MR = MC determines quantity. Price is read off the demand curve. Profit = (P − ATC) × Q.

In long-run equilibrium, free entry/exit drives economic profit to zero. The demand curve shifts until it is tangent to ATC — the "tangency condition." This creates excess capacity and a markup over marginal cost, causing deadweight loss.

Unlike perfect competition, long-run equilibrium does not occur at minimum ATC. The gap is the cost of product variety.
